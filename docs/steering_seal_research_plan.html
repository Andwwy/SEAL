General versus task-specific reasoning-calibration vectors

Using SEAL's recipe — contrast execution thoughts against over-thinking — does one general calibration vector recover the per-domain accuracy and token-efficiency gains of separate math, code, and logic vectors? And is it a linear combination of them, or a distinct direction?

no fine-tuning DeepSeek-R1-Distill reasoner extract = evaluate dual metric: accuracy + tokens
What changed from v1. We replace the CAA positive/negative recipe with SEAL's contrast — S = H̄E − H̄RT (execution vs. reflection ∪ transition), read at the \n\n thought boundaries at one layer and applied at every boundary during decoding. The research questions are unchanged; only the vector recipe — and the addition of a token-efficiency metric — is new.
01

Research questions

The same three questions as v1 — recovery, geometry, specificity — now asked of SEAL-style calibration vectors, and now over two outcomes (accuracy and tokens).

Primary research question

When a single general reasoning-calibration vector is extracted (SEAL recipe) from a mixture of reasoning tasks, how much of each task-specific vector's accuracy and token-efficiency gain does it recover — and is the general vector a linear combination of the task-specific vectors, or a distinct direction?

Q1

Recovery. How much of each task-specific vector's home-benchmark gain — in both accuracy and token reduction — does the pooled general vector recover, and at what cost?

Q2

Geometry. Is the general vector well explained by the span of the three task-specific vectors, or does a non-trivial residual remain — a shared calibration structure beyond a linear combination?

Q3

Specificity. Do task-specific vectors help their home benchmark while degrading others, and does negating a vector (−S) re-introduce over-thinking, as it should if the direction is meaningful?

A steering vector is a direction added to a model's internal activity at inference time to shift its behaviour, with no retraining. In SEAL's case the behaviour is "stay on task": the vector points from the average over-thinking state toward the average execution state.

We build a specific calibration vector per skill — math, code, logic — and one general vector from all three pooled. The primary question then splits into two measurable halves.

  • The trade-off (Q1). If we build one general vector from a mix of all three skills, how much of each specific vector's accuracy and token-saving does it keep, and can one vector substitute for three?
  • The geometry (Q2). Is that general vector simply the three specific vectors added together, or a distinct direction? Given SEAL's reported math→code transfer, we expect a small residual — but quantifying it is the point.
02

Background and key references

The method we now adopt, the conceptual foundation, the extraction principle, and the work we differentiate from.

the method

SEAL: Steerable Reasoning Calibration

2025 · arXiv 2504.07986 · COLM

Builds a training-free steering vector S = H̄E − H̄RT by contrasting execution thoughts with reflection/transition thoughts at \n\n boundaries, then adds it at decode time.

Our new recipe for every vector, so they're all comparable — and the source of our dual metric.
the foundation

Editing Models with Task Arithmetic

Ilharco et al., 2022 · arXiv 2212.04089

Showed that in a model's weights, adding two task vectors gives a model good at both, and negating one removes that skill.

We ask the same composition question one level down, in activation space.
extract it right

ATLAS: Adaptive Test-Time Latent Steering

2026 · arXiv 2601.03093

Extracts reasoning steering vectors from actual benchmark reasoning traces, not role descriptions, and obtains real accuracy gains.

The template for matching extraction to evaluation — which SEAL also follows.
what we test

Steer2Adapt

2026 · arXiv 2602.07276

Composes steering vectors and treats a general capability as a combination of domain directions — but assumes the combination works.

We don't assume it. Testing whether the general calibration vector is a combination is our hook.
03

Methodology

How each vector is built with SEAL's contrast — identical recipe everywhere, only the data domain changes.

With the recipe fixed, we build five vectors on a DeepSeek-R1-Distill reasoner (layer 20, α≈1.0 as SEAL defaults; both confirmed by ablation):

Why a reasoning model is required. SEAL's contrast only exists if the model produces reflection/transition thoughts. A vanilla 8B does not — so we use a distilled reasoner (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B; the Llama-8B distill is the drop-in if we want the "8B" framing). All thoughts are segmented at \n\n and labelled by SEAL's keyword list (their Appendix B).

Three controls are built in. Flipping a vector's sign (−S) should worsen the matching benchmark (more over-thinking); a random direction should do little; and a shuffled-label vector (execution/over-thinking labels assigned at random before averaging) should give ≈0 — proving it is the contrast, not any activation difference, carrying the signal.

04

Experimental design

A dual-metric effect matrix on held-out test data, plus three analyses that answer Q1–Q3.

Recovery% (per domain d)  =  gain( S_general , d )  ÷  gain( S_d , d )   ·   computed for accuracy and for token reduction

What we measure

  • Recovery %. Of a specific vector's home gain — in accuracy and in tokens — how much does S_general keep?
  • In-span residual. ‖r‖ / ‖S_general‖ after least-squares projection onto {S_math, S_code, S_logic}, plus the full cosine matrix.
  • Trade-off. Each vector's home gain vs. its average effect on the other two skills.

How we keep it honest

  • Anti, random & shuffled-label controls (from §03).
  • Matched tuning. Pick α and the layer on a validation split, report on a held-out test split — extraction set ⊂ train, no overlap, so no vector wins by lucky tuning.
  • Greedy decoding for reproducibility (sampling in appendix), exactly as SEAL.

The headline artifact is the effect matrix: 7 vectors × 3 benchmarks, every cell measured twice — change in accuracy and change in token count vs. the unsteered baseline.

06

Anticipated results

Illustrative shapes only — the figures the experiments are designed to produce, not measured data.

Every outcome is informative. Given SEAL's reported transfer, the likely result is that S_general ≈ S_combo with a small residual — establishing that the reasoning-calibration direction is largely domain-general, in contrast to skill/content directions which tend to be domain-specific. If instead S_general exceeds the combination or falls outside the plane, calibration carries shared structure the parts do not capture. Both settle the open question.
